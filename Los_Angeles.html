Los Angeles

The first group of people to ever explore the area of what is known as Los Angeles were the Native American tribes Chumash and Tongva.It wasn't until 1781, when the Spanish came over and settled in the area. Mexico would later take ownership of the city after they gained independence from Spain.It would later be given to the US where it would be declared an offical city in 1847.


Los Angeles would soon grow to be the 2nd most populated American city accoring to Britannica with about 3.8 million people living there. LA is a multicultral city with alot of interesting neighborhoods offering you a glimpse into different cultures such as Koreatown, Boyle heights, and Chinatown. If you want more information on these parts of Los Angeles check at the bottom half of this webpage. A big reason to check out Los Angeles is the variation of all the different foods you can eat within the city. When you're walking down the street in just about any part of town, you can find a Chinese resturant, not to far away from an Indian resturant or a Mexican resturant. Some of the local favorites include In-n-out and King Taco. If you are fan of cinema you can check out the TCL Chinese theather located in Hollywood Blvd or visit Universal Studios which is an entire theme park based on movies.


In Koreatown you can find alot of resturants and places representing the Korean cultural.In the neigborhood you will see alot of Korean owned businesses as well as tasty cuisine to fill you up such as the Kobawoo House which is rated 4.5 out of 5 stars on Google.

Boyle Heights is a neigborhood with alot of history behind as its one of the earliest neigborhoods in LA. Its a melting pot of cultures within the neigborhood as their are Japanese, Mexican, and Armenian communities here. One of the most famous spots to visit here is the Mariachi Plaza which is Mexican pueblo inspired by the architecture and a hang out spot for tourist or locals.

Chinatown brings you a piece of China's culture into California. The art style of this neighboorhod will capture your eye, since there are many murals as well as traditional red latterns and golden dragons decorated all over the neigborhood. The Chinatown Central Plaza is a popular meet up space where you can shop, eat, or just explore.
